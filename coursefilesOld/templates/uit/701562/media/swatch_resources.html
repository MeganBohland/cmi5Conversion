WEBSITES

Military Transcripts:

  • Verification of Military Experience and Training (VMET) documentation
    https://pki.dmdc.osd.mil/tgps/pages/VMET/overview.xhtml
  • Joint Services Transcript
    https://jst.doded.mil/smart/signIn.do
    Available March 2013 as a unified transcript for training acquired across all the Service branches.

Employment Statistics

  • U.S. Department of Labor’s Bureau of Labor Statistics
    http://bls.gov/
    National and state statistics on inflation and prices, unemployment, employment, productivity, pay and benefits, and more.

Certifications

  • Credentialing Opportunities Online (COOL)
    • Army: https://www.cool.army.mil/
    • Navy: https://www.cool.navy.mil/
    • Marine Corps: https://www.cool.navy.mil/usmc/
    Information about credentialing and licensing. Use COOL to get background information about civilian credentials; identify licenses and certifications relevant to Army, Navy, and Marine Corps MOSs; learn how to fill gaps between training and experience and civilian requirements; and learn about resources that can help soldiers gain civilian job credentials.
  • Defense Activity for Non-Traditional Education Support (DANTES)
    http://www.dantes.doded.mil/
    Expands personal education for Service members and Veterans through partnerships across the Department of Defense and the education community.
  • Guard Apprenticeship Program Initiative (GAPI)
    http://www.nationalguard.com/tools/guard-apprenticeship-program-initiative
    Army National Guard and Reserve Component Soldiers can use their military skills on the way to a civilian career. When you complete your apprenticeship, you’ll be fully certified to work in your chosen occupation or trade.

    Working as an apprentice gives you on-the-job training and a paycheck while you earn nationally recognized certification in a specialized field. There are over 100 military occupational specialties approved by the Department of Labor as apprenticeship trades.

O*NET Family of Sites

  • O*NET Online (main job seeker site)
    http://www.onetonline.org/
    Site for job seekers has Attn: Veterans link, advanced search options, and a portal to various crosswalks; sponsored by the U.S. Department of Labor and part of the American Job Center network.
  • My Next Move for Veterans
    http://www.mynextmove.org/vets
    Link to the O*NET Interest Profiler assessment; also three ways to search civilian careers including the MOC Crosswalk.
  • My Next Move (general job seekers)
    http://www.mynextmove.org/
    For all job seekers, including military spouses. Search for careers by keywords or by industry, or take the Interest Profiler.

Other Job Seekers Sites

  • Veterans Employment Center (VEC)
    https://www.ebenefits.va.gov/ebenefits/jobs
    eBenefits is a portal; a central location for Veterans, Service Members, and their families to research, find, access, and, in time, manage their benefits and personal information.
  • Career One Stop
    http://www.careeronestop.org/
    Sponsored by the U.S. Department of Labor, offering tools to help job seekers, students, businesses, and career professionals—includes resumes and interviewing advice.
  • Hero 2 Hired
    https://h2h.jobs/
    Provides comprehensive career readiness to support Reserve Component Services members and their spouses by delivering informative training and assisting with key career preparation activities such as building resume, performing effective job searches, interviewing and networking.
  • Veterans Administration (VA for Vets)
    http://vaforvets.va.gov/
    Facilitates the reintegration, retention, and hiring of Veteran employees at the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA). Offers career-search tools for Veterans seeking employment at VA and elsewhere; sponsors hiring events; offers a military skills translator; offers coaching and reintegration support for military Service members.
  • U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs VetSuccess
    http://www.vetsuccess.gov/
    One-stop site for veteran and Service member employment needs. Browse job listings and post resumes.
