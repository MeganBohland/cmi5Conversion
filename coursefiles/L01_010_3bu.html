Why Trafficking in Persons Occurs

Experts agree that trafficking in persons occurs for many reasons including:

  • The worldwide demand for commercial sex or cheap labor
  • Economic or political instability, war, civil unrest, and natural disasters which create large scale migration and other vulnerabilities
  • Criminal enterprises such as terrorist organizations, organized crime, and extremist groups who use human trafficking to fund their operations

Victims can be:

  • Any sex, age, race, nationality, social status, economic background, or immigration status
  • Female or male
  • Adult or child
  • Foreign national or U.S. citizen
  • Homeless youth
  • Undocumented migrants
  • People displaced by civil conflicts and natural disasters
  • Service members, DoD civilians, DoD contractor employees, and DoD family members

Traffickers can be:

  • Any sex, age, race, nationality, social status, economic background, ethnicity, or immigration status
  • Members of organized crime groups
  • Terrorist organizations
  • Gangs and warlords
  • Pimps
  • Business owners
  • Family members
  • Service members, DoD civilians, DoD contractors, and DoD family members
